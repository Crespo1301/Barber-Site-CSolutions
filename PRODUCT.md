# PRODUCT.md

## Register

**brand**, this site IS the product. It is a sellable, ready-to-deploy template marketed by CSolutions to barbers who currently run their entire web presence through a Calendly or Booksy link.

## Product Purpose

A universal, premium, drop-in barber site template. Sold as a finished asset: buyer pays, CSolutions swaps in their name, photos, services, hours, and booking link. Goal of every screen is to make a barber look like they already have a high-end brand, even if today they only have a phone and a chair.

## Users

**Primary buyers:**
- Solo barbers building a personal brand (booth renters, traveling barbers, suite operators)
- Shop owners with 2–8 chairs who outgrew their Booksy/Calendly link and want a real homepage

**Shared traits:**
- Mobile-first audience. Most clients find them on Instagram, click a link in bio.
- Image-driven. Their work is the proof. Galleries, before/afters, headshots matter more than copy.
- Trust signals matter: clean cuts, consistent fades, military discount, regulars, walk-in policy.
- Time-poor. They will not edit a complex site. Template must look complete with minimal copy.

**Secondary buyer:** CSolutions itself, marketing this template publicly as a portfolio/lead piece.

## Tone

Street but luxurious. High-end barbershop, not corporate, not nightclub, not country-club. Confident, lived-in, masculine without being aggressive. Talks like a barber who knows their craft, not a brand manager.

Words it would say: precision, edge-up, taper, lineup, walk-in, regular, by appointment.
Words it would not say: solutions, experience, journey, elevate, redefine.

## Anti-references (what this is NOT)

- Red-and-white barber pole stripes. Wood-grain backgrounds. Vintage chair stock photos. Old-timey "SHAVE & A HAIRCUT" western typography.
- Cheap Squarespace/Wix barber templates with a hero photo of someone else's shop and a $9 stock fade.
- SaaS-cream "AI workflow tool" aesthetic, pale grey cards, lavender gradients, rounded everything.
- Black + gold luxury cliché. Casino-tier. Already saturated.
- Generic dark mode "tech" feel. This is a barbershop, not an analytics dashboard.

## Reference Direction

Aimé Leon Dore lookbooks. Bode editorial pages. Supreme product drops. Frank Ocean Blonded site. Magazine-grade barber shoots in `Inventory` or `Mr Porter Journal`. Type-forward, photo-heavy, generous whitespace, one strong accent.

## Strategic Principles

1. **The work is the hero.** Photography always wins over copy. Layouts must make even mediocre phone-shot fades look intentional.
2. **Sellable on day one.** Every section must read as finished without the buyer's content. Placeholder copy must sound real, not `Lorem ipsum`. Placeholder photos must be styled, not random.
3. **One booking action.** No competing CTAs. The site funnels to one thing: book.
4. **Editable in one file.** All buyer-specific content lives in `src/data/site.js`. Buyer never touches JSX.
5. **Mobile is the canvas.** Design mobile first; desktop is the upscale.
6. **Confidence over cuteness.** No mascots, no cartoon scissors, no "barber bro" jokes.

## Surfaces in scope

Single-page site: Hero → About → Services → Gallery (before/after) → Discount (military) → Contact → Footer. Sticky Navbar.
